Firearm Training

Gun Usage: All guns in Thunia tend to operate on the basic mechanics of load a round, fire a round, eject casing and other debris, repeat. If any event in that process fails, the gun misfires. When making an Attack Roll, if the d20 lands on a 1 or a 2, the gun Misfires. If the gun is in perfect condition, it gains the broken condition. If the gun has the broken condition, the gun explodes. All creatures in a 10ft radius of the gunslinger, including the gunslinger, must roll a DC15 DEX save or take the gun's regular damage.

There are ways your misfire chance can increase, such as using poorly made bullets, which means if your misfire chance is a 4, then when making an attack roll, a 4 or lower on the die, will cause a misfire.

Ammo: All ammunition is not created equal. Ammunition made for a pistol will not work for any other type of firearm. If a firearm is loaded and discharged with the incorrect ammunition, the weapon automatically misfires.

Reloading: All weapons have a magazine capacity, or how many times you can pull the trigger before you're out of ammunition in the weapon. Reloading, unless otherwise stated, takes a bonus action.

Repairs & Cleanings: A gunslinger needs to know their tools inside and out. With weapons only recently emerging in Thunia, having this knowledge is critical to a gunslinger's survival. During a short or long rest, you can repair your firearm back to it's original glory, with the aid of a set of Tinker's Tools. This cleaing and repair removes the broken condition as well as any effect that effect the efficacy of your firearm.

Ranges: Firearms have two ranges. The first is your Close Range. This is the range at which you are most effective at hitting targets. Anything past that is the Effective Range. This is the range that you are still capable of hitting within, but it is much harder. When firing at a target outside of your effective range, roll with disadvantage.

Gun Types: There are three main types of guns. For the purposes of defining the use case of these weapons, I will use the following metric;

  • Pistols: One Handed, Low to High Caliber Slug Round
  • Rifles: Two Handed, Low Caliber Slug Round
  • Snipers: Two Handed, High Caliber Slug Round
  • Shotguns: Two or One Handed, Spread Round

Muntions Creation

Since your ammunition is rare, and often difficult to come by, you need to be able to make your own. This isn't helped by the fact that once a Munition is discharged, it cannot be recovered after combat. With the aid of your Tinker's tools, and enough raw materials, you can create your own ammunition. You can create up to Gunslinger Level + Intelligence modifier rounds per hour of rest, with a minimum of 1 per hour.

Fighting Style

You adopt a particular fighting style that suits how you weild your weapon. Choose one of the following. You cannot take the same Fighting Style option more than once.

  • Precise Shot: +2 to attack rolls made with a firearm
  • Power Shot: When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage dice, reroll the die and take the new roll
  • Deulist: Sheathing or Drawing your weapon is a free action, and +2 to Initiative Rolls
  • Rough & Tumble: +1 to AC
  • Tough: When rolling HP, you may take a 6 on the die, instead of the regular method of rolling.
  • Double Trouble: You are proficient with using an offhand attack. Add your ability modifer to your off hand weapon.
    Can only dual weild with Pistols

Weapon of Choice

At 3rd level, you become more familiar with your subclasses weapon. This familiarity grants you the following bonuses:

  • Reloading with your weapon of choice is a free action, instead of a bonus action
  • Your misfire chance is 1 with your weapon of choice
  • When creating Ammunition, add your proficiency bonus when determining how much ammunition is made.

Armed with Magics

At 10th level, you infuse your weapon with a special magical property. As part of an attack, you execute the following skill according to your weapon. This effect lasts 2d6+2 rounds. This can be done a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier.

  • Pistols: For the duration, you gain an additional attack, and an additional 10 ft of movement speed.
  • Rifles: You infuse your weapon with Magical Energy. Choose one of the following Damage types; Acid, Fire, Cold, Lightning, or Poison. For the duration, your weapon deals an additional dice of damage of that given elemental type.
  • Sniper: For the duration, you ignore all range penalties imposed by your weapon. As long as there is no obstruction between you and the target, you make the attack as normal. All of your damage from your gun is considered Force Damage.
  • Shotguns: Choose one of the following damage types; Acid, Fire, Cold, Lightning, or Poison. When the trigger is pulled, the gun fires off a 30 ft cone, of your chosen damage type. Each creature in that area must make a Dexterity saving throw, taking 3d6 damage, or half as much on a success.

At 3rd level, you gain proficiency with your explosives kit, called a Demo Kit. This Kit allows you to create destructive equipment during your downtime. Each type of Bomb comes with special properties of destruction. Every Explosive uses your Save DC. Your Save DC is calculated as 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Intelligence modifier.

Your Demo Kit's Bombs are expendable at any rate you wish. So long as you have the bomb, there is no limit to your destructive power. Once a bomb is placed, it is primed for detonation. You can trigger a detonation of all primed bombs as a free action. The deonation range is noted on each bomb as Trigger Distance.

Firearm Creation (Optional)

Firearms are an immense leap forward in Technology. As such, incorporating them into your games can bring a lot of scrutiny as to their function, creation, and features. This section attempts to explain their creation and general function. I leave the features and facets to your imagination.

The following is the creation process of a firearm from start to finish. All steps can be done completely seperate. Your Gun can be disassembled and rebuilt as part of the clean procedure, so you're free to rebuild your Gun as you see fit.

  1. Barrel Casting
  2. Stock Casting/Whittling
  3. Ammo Tinkering
  4. Receiver Tinkering
  5. Final Assembly

For the steps that have you roll on a Success table, you calculate the success with the following formula:

Success = 1d100 + Gunslinger Level + Tool Proficiency + Dexterity + Success Modifer
Barrel Casting
Requirements: Active Forge + Smith's Tools + Tinker's Tools

Casting is the process of forging material and placing the molten material into a cast, which is then used to create the barrel. This part needs to be the most hardy of the firearm, as it's the piece that needs to withstand the most abuse, and remain true. There are several different types of materials that can be used to create a Barrel, each with different properties.

When crafting a Barrel, you'll need an active forge for a few hours. To begin creating a barrel, choose your material, spend the requisite time building the barrel, and finally roll on the success table. At the end of this process, you have a part that's worthy of being attached to a gun.

  • Pistol: 2 Hours + 2 lbs of Material
  • Rifle & Shotgun: 3 Hours + 5 lbs of Material
  • Sniper: 4 Hours + 8 lbs of Material
Note: 1 Hour is allotted to prep work during a long rest.

Final Assembly
Requirements: Tinker's Tools

Final Assembly is the part you've been looking forward to. After all, it's when you finally get your Gun to a usable state. To begin final assembly, ensure all of your parts are compatible. Finally, assemble them together with your Tinker's Tools. You can also dismantle them with this step, to rebuild a new weapon with old parts.

  • Pistol: 20 Minutes
  • Rifle & Shotgun: 30 Minutes
  • Sniper: 45 Minutes
Note: 1 Hour is allotted to prep work during a long rest.
