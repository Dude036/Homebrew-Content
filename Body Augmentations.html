Body Augmentations

A world isn't truly steampunk until people are using mechanical arms and legs for no reason. If you want to do this as well, here's how you can do that! There is however an important distinction to be made with what defines an Augmentation vs an Override.

Augmentation: A change to one's body that retains it's current functionality and base. Does not consitute a replacement, but an addition. If an Augment break, wears out, or drains its battery, you can still function normally.

Override: A replacement to the body's natural solution to the problem. This means that everything has been replaced, and a new feature has been put there. If an Override breaks, wears out, or drains its battery, the part is now just dead weight attached to your body. Each Section will define their Broken condition. If your Override breaks, wears out, or drains its battery, it is considered Broken, and you take the penalty associated with it.

Battery

Every Override, and some augmentations require a battery to be used to power themselves. Assume that every augment and override on every list requires a battery, unless otherwise stated. There are three types of batteries: Standard Rechargable, Premium Rechargable, and Soul Batteries.

  • Standard Rechargable:
    Max Charges Held: 15
    Spell Slots Accepted: 1 - 9
    Daily Recharge Amount: 0
    Battery Cost: 100 gp

    Standard Rechargable is the lowest model. If you use a charge on the augment, you do not automatically get it back. It is removed, and can only be returned by having a caster use a spell slot to recharge it.

  • Premium Rechargable:
    Max Charges Held: 10
    Spell Slots Accepted: 1 - 5
    Daily Recharge Amount: 1d2, 1d3, 1d4, or 1d6
    Battery Cost: 1,000 gp

    Premium Rechargable is the model everyone wants. When this type of augment is not in active use, it recharges itself from the Weave, albeit slowly. The cap for this battery isn't as high, since it requires an additional module to handle recharging itself.

  • Soul Rechargable:
    Max Charges Held: 20
    Spell Slots Accepted: 1 - 9
    Daily Recharge Amount: 1d4, 1d6, 1d8, or 1d10
    Battery Cost: 10,000 gp

    Soul Rechargable toes the fine line between innovation and madness. This augment battery uses the souls of captured creatures or people to power itself. As a result, sometime it can be contacted through magical means, sometimes intentionally, and sometimes not.
