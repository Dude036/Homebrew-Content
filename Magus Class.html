Magus Class

The Magus is not someone who would study from old tomes in a musty library, or sit in a classroom full of pompous wizards. The Magus trains not only their mind, but their body as well. The secrets of magic for them, are told through plunging their steel into the flesh of their enemies.

Arcane Pool

Every magus contains a pool of magical energy within themselves. You can tap into this pool of power to enhance your abilities, to enchant weaponry or armor, and to shield yourself from magical effects. Your Arcane Pool pool is equal to half your level + your Intelligence modifier.

At first level, you can use your Arcane Pool to do the following;

  • Enchant: As a free action, use 1 point from your Arcane Pool to enchant your weapon to become a +1 weapon. If your weapon is already enchanted to be +1 or greater, this property is added to it as well. i.e. a +2 weapon, that is enchanted via your Arcane pool points, becomes a +3 weapon.

Spellcasting

You have become a student of arcane magic. Your lifeline to magic is in your spellbook. At first level, your spellbook contains a number of spells equal to your magus level + your Intelligence modifier. At each level up, you gain an additional spell that is on the Wizards' spell list.

Your Spellbook is not a stagnant thing. It is ever changing. You may add spells to your spell book by copying them down into your spellbook. This process take 2 hours and 50 gold per spell level. You can then choose to prepare this spell as you would any other spell that you know. You are also able to copy down any spell from a spell scroll using this method. In order to do so, the spell must also be on the Wizard spell list.

Spell Combat

Your prowess on the battlefield may not yet be tested, however your aptitude is more inline than what is normally expected. You gain the following benefits;

  • While not encumbered, you gain an 10 feet of additional movement speed.
  • You gain a bonus to Constitution saving throws for maintaining concentration. The bonus is equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum +1).
  • So long as you have a free hand, you can treat your open hand as if were an arcane focus.

Intelligent Dodge

While you are wearing no armor, and weilding no shield, your AC is equal to your Dexterity bonus + your Intelligence Bonus.

Spell Strike

Your weapon is now the vehicle for your spellcasting. If you have one free hand, you may spend your bonus action to imbue your weapon with a spell whose casting time is no longer than 1 action. All spells that you imbue into your weapon have their range reduced to touch, unless the spell has a range of self. If the spell has an area of effect and allows for a save, you have advantage on that save.

If you are unable to trigger your spells effect on the turn it was cast, at the start of your next turn, you can use your bonus action to attempt to maintain concentration on the spell. The DC for maintaining concentraition is equal to 10 + the spell's level.

At 3rd level, you choose a Magus Arcana from the list available that you strive to emulate in your combat styles and techniques. The archetype you choose grants you features at 3rd level and again at 7th, 10th, 15th, and 18th level. Here is a list of Magus Arcana that you can choose from;

  • Weapon Specialist
  • Arcane Specialist

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

If your DM allows the use of feats, you may instead take a feat.

Enhanced Arcane Pool Usage

Your arcane pool has increased usability. You may now expend Arcane pool points to attune your weapon with any number of the following properties as a free action

Dancing
Prerequisite: Melee weapon
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 3 point(s)

You release your weapon to float about and attack for itself. Your weapon says afloat for a number of rounds equal to your proficiency bonus. If the weapon is held by the end of it's Dancing lifespan, the weapon drops to the ground. This weapon attacks using your attack bonus and deals damage as if you were using it. You can use your bonus action to command the weapon to move up to 20 feet. If the weapon is on your square or adjacent to you, you can grasp the weapon as a free action. The weapon cannot make attacks of Opportunity while dancing.

Flaming
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 1 point(s)

This weapon is covered in fire. Attacks made with this weapon deal an additional 1d6 of fire damage.

Flaming Burst
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 2 point(s)

This weapon is covered in fire. Attacks made with this weapon deal an additional 1d6 of fire damage. If the wielder makes a critical hit against a creature, the damage becomes 1d10, instead of 1d6. If this weapon's critical is greater than x2, it deals 1d10 extra damage for every number above 2. x3 = 2d10; x7 = 6d10.

Frost
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 1 point(s)

This weapon is covered in ice. Attacks made with this weapon deal an additional 1d6 of cold damage.

Icy Burst
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 2 point(s)

This weapon is covered in ice. Attacks made with this weapon deal an additional 1d6 of cold damage. If the wielder makes a critical hit against a creature, the damage becomes 1d10, instead of 1d6. If this weapon's critical is greater than x2, it deals 1d10 extra damage for every number above 2. x3 = 2d10; x7 = 6d10.

Keen
Prerequisite: Slashing or Piercing Melee Weapon
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 1 point(s)

This weapon's critical range is doubled. Ex. 19-20 [Range: 2] becomes 17-20 [Range: 4]

Shock
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 1 point(s)

This weapon crackles with electricity. Attacks made with thise weapon deal an additional 1d6 points of Lightning damage.

Shocking Burst
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 1 point(s)

This weapon crackles with electricity. Attacks made with thise weapon deal an additional 1d6 points of Lightning damage. If the wielder makes a critical hit against a creature, the damage becomes 1d10, instead of 1d6. If this weapon's critical is greater than x2, it deals 1d10 extra damage for every number above 2. x3 = 2d10; x7 = 6d10

Speed
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 3 point(s)

This weapon is swift. The user gains an additional attack when taking the Attack Action with this weapon.

Vorpal
Prerequisite: Slashing Melee weapon
Arcane Pool Point Cost: 5 point(s)

This weapon decapitates the target. This weapon cannot benefit from abilites that increase it's critical range. On a Critical hit, make another attack roll. If the second attack roll surpasses the AC of the target, the target is dead. On a fail, treat the critical normally.

* Note: In order to enchant your weapon with the above properties, The weapon must already be enchanted to a +1 weapon already. This can be achieved by have the weapon enhanced at a blacksmith, or by Enchanting it yourself as part of using your Arcane Pool Points, as describe at level 1.

Extra Attack

Beginning at 5th level, you can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

The number of attacks increases to three when you reach 14th level in this class.

Medium Armor Training

Beginning at 7th level, you have trained enough to upgrade to heavier and stiffer armor.

Arcane Pool Recovery

Beginning at 7th level, when you reduce a foe to below 0 HP, you regain 1 arcane pool point. This ability cannot increase your current arcane pool points above the maximum amount of arcane pool points you can have.

Evasion

Beginning at 7th level, you can nimbly dodge out of the way of certain area effects, such as a red dragon's fiery breath or an ice storm spell. When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

Ability Score Improvement

Ultimate Arcane Pool

When you reach 9th level, your Arcane pool now has the potential to upgrade a normal weapon far beyond it's normal potential. Your Enchant ability is now upgraded to the following ability

Enchant: As a free action, use up to 5 points from your Arcane Pool to enchant your weapon to become up to a +5 weapon. If your weapon is already enchanted to be +1 or greater, this property is added to it as well. i.e. a +2 weapon, that is enchanted in this way can become up to a +7 weapon.

Versitile Genius Strike

When you're making a damage roll, you can choose to either use your Strength, Dexterity, or Intelligence modifier as an additive.

Magus Arcana Feature

At 18th level, you choose a Magus Arcana from the list available that you strive to emulate in your combat styles and techniques.

Spell Mastery

At 18th level, you have achieved such mastery over certain spells that you can cast them at will. Choose a 1st-level magus spell and a 2nd-level magus spell that are in your spellbook. You can cast those spells at their lowest level without expending a spell slot when you have them prepared. If you want to cast either spell at a higher level, you must expend a spell slot as normal.

By spending 8 hours in study, you can exchange one or both of the spells you chose for different spells of the same levels.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 20th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

If your DM allows the use of feats, you may instead take a feat.

Weapon of Legend

When you reach 20th level, your weapon is written in books as a thing of legend. Any weapon that you weild automatically is treated as a +2 weapon as if you've enchanted it with your Arcane Pool. Your Enchant ability from your Arcane pool point is now upgraded to the following.

Enchant: As a free action, use up to 8 points from your Arcane Pool to enchant your weapon to become up to a +8 weapon. If your weapon is already enchanted to be +1 or greater, this property is added to it as well. i.e. a +2 weapon, that is enchanted in this way can become up to a +10 weapon

Weapon Specialist

Your hands no longer are free, but instead filled with deadly instruments of death. In you mind, a Knife in the gut, is faster, easier, and more deadly than adding some acid to your weapon.

Full Hands

At 3rd level, you no longer require a free hand to cast spells as a Magus normally would. Choose one of the following fighting styles as your mastery, and gain it's effect;

  • Two-handed Weapons

    When dealing damage with your weapon, add 1 and 1/2 your Strength modifier.

  • Dual Weilding

    You may draw and store both of your weapons, ina ny scenario where you could draw a weapon.

  • Crossbows / Bows

    Your Spellstrike range for Bows allows you to strike an enemy within your range incriment, or the spells range, whichever is closer.

  • Shielded

    Your Intelligent Dodge feature now allows for the weilding of a shield.

Advanced Martial Training

At 7th Level, gain the following feature depending on what fighting style you chose;

  • Two-handed Weapon: While weilding a two-handed weapon, you can spend an Arcane pool point to overpower your attack. All attacks made that round deal additional damage equal to your Strength modifier.
  • Dual Wielding: While dual weilding, you can use an Arcane pool point to double your movement speed until the end of your turn.
  • Shielded: While holding a shield, you can use an Arcane pool point to reflect physical damage back at a melee attacker. As a Reaction, make an attack roll against the creature who attacked you. If the attack hits, split the incoming damage between you and the target who attacked you.
  • Crossbows / Bow: While firing from a bow, you may target a square that an enemy is not occupying. Treat it as a creature of AC 5.

Hit Harder

At 10th level, gain the following feature depending on what fighting style you chose;

  • Two-handed Weapon: When using the Attack action, you can use an Arcane pool point to redirect the attack to an adjacent foe. You must make a seperate attack roll against the new creature's AC.
  • Dual Wielding When using the Attack action, you can make an additional attack with your offhand weapon. Your offhand attack does not use your Strength modifier when calculating damage.
  • Shielded: As a Free action, you can spend 1 arcane pool point fo gain Resistance to one Physical damage type for 1 round. This cannot be used more than once per turn.
  • Crossbows / Bow: As a part of an attack action, you can choose to expend an arcane pool point to add your Intelligence modifier to your attack roll until the end of your turn.

Arcane Knowledge

At 15th level, When making a skill check on a skill you have no proficiency with, you can use an arcane pool point to gain temporary proficiency in that skill for 1 minute.

Ultimate Martial Actions

At 18th level, gain the following feature depending on what fighting style you chose;

  • Two-handed Weapon: Gain the following Action:
    Cleave
    You can make one attack against all creatures within reach of your weapon. Each attack roll is made seperately, and damage from this attack takes a cumulative -2 for each subsequent attack. i.e. the 2nd person hit by this attack take -2 damage, the 3rd taking -4, etc.
  • Dual Wielding: Gain the following Action:
    Onslaught
    You attack with the fury of a wild animal. Make all of your attacks simultaneously. If any of the attacks made as part of this action are critical hits, treat all the damage as if every attack was also critical.
  • Shielded: If you do not move on your turn, your Shield's AC bonus is doubled until the start of your next turn.
  • Crossbows / Bows: Gain the following action
    Graze
    While aiming at a target, you may choose to graze them, aming at all creatures within that line. Make an attack roll, on a hit you dealing minimum damage to the target, releasing any spell cast via Spell Strike, and the arrow continues on in a line. If it encounters another target make another attack roll. On a hit, it deals minimum damage. This effect continues to the full range of your weapon.
